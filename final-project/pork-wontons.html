Pork Wontons

Whether it's the new year or just the weekend, sit down with your family and wrap some wontons. Easily store them in the freezer and satisfy your wonton cravings any time.

Ingredients

6 Servings

1 lb Ground pork
5 stalks Green onion
2 tbsp Soy sauce
1 tbsp Sesame oil
1 knob Ginger, minced
2 tbsp Cooking wine
0.25 tbsp Salt
72 Wonton wrappers

Instructions

  1. Add the ground pork, ginger, green onion, salt, sesame oil, cooking wine, and soy sauce to a large bowl. Mix until the ingredients are combined.
  2. Place 1 teaspoon of the meat mixture in the middle of a wrapper.
  3. Dip your finger in a bowl of water and run it along the edges of the wonton wrapper. Fold the wrapper over the meat mixture to form a triangle. Seal the wet edges together.
  4. Fold the two bottom corners in and seal them with water pressing firmly.
  5. Cover with a damp paper towel and repeat the steps to form all wontons.
  6. When you're ready to cook the wontons, put 6 cups of water into a pot and bring the water to a boil.
  7. When the water comes to a boil, place wontons into the boiling water.
  8. When the wontons float to the top, the wontons should be cooked. You can check the inside of one of them to make sure the meat is fully cooked.
  9. Take them out gently with a strainer and serve them hot with a drizzle of soy sauce.
  10. Repeat the cooking process with the rest of the wontons until they're all cooked.
